# GoTravel — Voice & Tone Guide

## Brand Voice

GoTravel sounds like **a well-traveled friend who's genuinely excited to help you plan your next adventure**. We're knowledgeable but never condescending. Enthusiastic but never salesy. Casual but never careless.

## Voice Attributes

### 1. Adventurous
We inspire action. Our language creates a sense of possibility and excitement.

- **Do:** "Wake up to mist rolling over rice paddies in Sapa"
- **Don't:** "Visit scenic rice paddies"

### 2. Approachable
We talk like a real person. No corporate speak, no travel-industry jargon.

- **Do:** "Not sure where to start? We've got you."
- **Don't:** "Inquire with our team about bespoke itinerary curation."

### 3. Trustworthy
We're transparent and honest. We set real expectations — even when the truth is less glamorous.

- **Do:** "The trek is tough — 6 hours uphill — but the view at the top makes every step worth it."
- **Don't:** "Enjoy a leisurely jungle trek" (when it's actually brutal)

### 4. Inclusive
We make everyone feel welcome, regardless of travel experience, age, or background.

- **Do:** "Whether it's your first time in Asia or your tenth, this trip has something new to discover."
- **Don't:** "For experienced travelers only" / "Not for the faint of heart"

### 5. Warm
We genuinely care about our travelers and the communities we visit.

- **Do:** "Your guide Min grew up in this village — he'll introduce you to his family over a home-cooked lunch."
- **Don't:** "Includes local village visit and lunch."

## Tone by Context

The voice stays the same; the tone shifts depending on the situation.

| Context | Tone | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Trip descriptions** | Vivid, immersive, exciting | "Kayak through emerald lagoons, eat street food with locals, and sleep in a treehouse above the jungle canopy." |
| **Social media** | Casual, fun, conversational | "POV: You just traded your Monday meeting for a sunrise over Angkor Wat." |
| **Email marketing** | Warm, personal, helpful | "Hey Sarah — we noticed you were eyeing the Vietnam trip. Spots are filling up for October, and we'd hate for you to miss it." |
| **Blog posts** | Informative, storytelling, relatable | "I'll be honest — I almost skipped Luang Prabang. I'm glad I didn't. Here's why it became my favorite stop." |
| **Customer support** | Empathetic, clear, solution-oriented | "I totally understand — flight delays are stressful. Here's what we can do to adjust your itinerary." |
| **Ads (paid)** | Punchy, benefit-driven, urgent | "7 days. 3 countries. All-inclusive from $1,299. Book by Friday." |
| **Landing pages** | Clear, persuasive, benefit-focused | "Small groups. Local guides. No hidden fees. This is travel done right." |

## Writing Rules

### Sentence Structure
- Lead with the most interesting or important word
- Keep sentences under 20 words where possible
- Mix short punchy sentences with longer descriptive ones for rhythm
- Use fragments sparingly but intentionally: "Just you, the jungle, and the sound of the river."

### Paragraphs
- Max 3-4 sentences per paragraph in blogs and emails
- One idea per paragraph
- Use subheadings every 2-3 paragraphs in long-form content

### Punctuation & Formatting
- Use em dashes (—) for emphasis and asides, not parentheses
- Oxford comma: always (beaches, temples, and street food)
- Exclamation marks: max one per piece of content — overuse kills authenticity
- Ellipses: avoid in professional content; OK sparingly in social media
- ALL CAPS: never in body copy; OK in social captions for one word max

### Words We Love
| Use this | Instead of this |
|---|---|
| Adventure | Vacation / Holiday |
| Explore | Visit |
| Experience | Activity |
| Discover | See |
| Locals | Natives |
| Travelers | Tourists |
| Immersive | Interesting |
| Curated | Selected |
| Journey | Trip (OK in casual contexts) |

### Words & Phrases We Avoid
- "Once in a lifetime" — overused, loses meaning
- "Hidden gem" — every travel brand says this
- "Bucket list" — cliche
- "Exotic" — can feel othering
- "Third world" / "Developing country" — use the country name instead
- "Cheap" — say "affordable" or "great value"
- "Tourist trap" — say "overcrowded" or "overhyped"
- "No worries" — say "no stress" or "we've got you covered"

### Hashtag Strategy (Social Media)
- Max 15 hashtags on Instagram, placed in first comment — not caption
- Always include: #GoTravelAdventures #SmallGroupTravel
- Mix broad + niche: #SoutheastAsiaTravel + #SapaRiceTerracesHike
- Never use: #FollowForFollow, #InstaTravel, or spam-style tags

## CTA (Call to Action) Style

| Strength | When to use | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **Soft** | Blog posts, awareness content | "See all trips" / "Read more stories" |
| **Medium** | Email, social, trip pages | "Explore this trip" / "Check availability" |
| **Strong** | Ads, promos, limited availability | "Book now — only 4 spots left" / "Grab your spot before Friday" |

- Always use action verbs: Explore, Discover, Book, Join, Start
- Avoid generic CTAs: "Click here", "Submit", "Learn more" (unless paired with context)
- Match CTA urgency to content context — don't put "BOOK NOW" on a blog post
